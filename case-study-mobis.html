Hyundai Mobis AAOS

Wireframe - Prototype

Overview

As a key participant in the automotive industry, Hyundai Mobis, a prominent South Korean car company, is leading the charge in driving experience innovation. Luxoft, an Automotive Software Industry expert, secured the project to craft a cutting-edge in-vehicle interface for Mobis.
Employing Android Automotive Operating System (AAOS), a well-received model known for its smooth hardware integration and user-friendly interface tailored for driving tasks, we formulated our concept in alignment with AAOS principles.

My Role:

UX Designer & UX Researcher

Tools and Technique:

Mixed Methods

Timeline:

2021 June - 2021 October

Problems

Through research conducted by the Mobis UX team, it has shown that users are encountering numerous issues with the existing AAOS available in the market (elaborated in the process step1). Therefore, it was essential to develop a revised interface that addresses these concerns and offers an improved user experience.

Goal

“As a user, regardless of my age, I desire an in-car system that is user-friendly, ensuring that I can effortlessly access and control its features.”

Process

Discovery & Research — (Already done by Mobis UX team)

Users were experiencing the following pain points:

  • Difficulty in locating useful information within the Navigation App.
  • Needing to navigate through numerous clicks to access desired functions.
  • Frustration when having to go back to previous steps.
  • Facing a steep learning curve for the infotainment system.

Synthesis & Definition — Personas & User Journey Map

Tools: Miro, Sketch

  1. Proto Persona Creation: Guided by the client's defined target user group and their valuable input, I have built some Proto Personas as an anchor to guide our future concept development within a workshop.
  2. User Journey Mapping Workshop: Spanning three stages (departure, driving, and arrival), the workshop participants collaboratively outlined actions, touchpoints, customer thoughts, and feelings. I augmented this process by using the "How Might We" (HMW) brainstorming technique to explore potential opportunity areas for the concept.

Ideation & Testing — Wireframing & Prototyping

Tools: Sketch, ProtoPie

  1. Wireframing: Based on the input gathered during the workshop, we opted for an Android-themed digital concept as a promising avenue for exploration. I outlined the arrangement of distinct functions.
  2. Prototyping: Building on the Design team's digitized wireframes, we transitioned these into interactive prototypes for the purpose of usability testing.
  3. Usability Study: Participants engaged in usability tests, evaluating tasks like navigating the main screen, adjusting brightness, rearranging the app, and swapping out function widgets. This allowed us to access if the mobile-similar design could bring more familiarity and expedites the learning curve for users.
  4. Concept refinement: Based on the usability findings, I refined the wireframes and the prototypes for the subsequent handover phase.

Development & Delivery

UXD’s involvement concluded when we delivered the digital concept and prototypes to the development team.
One UX designer took over the responsibility of maintaining the user experience. They would assist the developers by explaining the concept, making adjustments as needed, and preparing software documentation.

Outcomes

In general, the AAOS concept was well-received by users, aligning with our expectations of providing a familiar experience. Nevertheless, our concept did have some actual flaws listed below, which we promptly updated before final delivery. The positive response from our customer was boosting our future collaboration to the next level.

What was good:

  • Users found the interface extremely intuitive
  • The HMI's resemblance to a phone contributed to a sense of familiarity.
  • The design itself perceived as simple and clear.
  • Users appreciated the ability to personalise their screens.

What should be improved (updated before delivery):

  • Some users struggled with finding their way back to the main screen (should be accomplished by swiping).
  • The placement of buttons led to confusion in using the Phone function.
  • Less familiarity with Google Assistant led to improper triggering of the voice assistant.
  • Many temperature indicators on the status bar caused confusion.
