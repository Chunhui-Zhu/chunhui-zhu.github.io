Upgrade the verification system on Atomichub

Community - UX Strategy

Overview

It is important for users to do their own research on Web3.0 world since scams are such a widespread problem for the industry. On the NFT Marketplace AtomicHub, there was a two-level system with checks (like the Twitter blue check) to refer the collection’s maturity level, which was manually reviewed by our customer support colleague. It performed as some protection layer especially for new users. However, we have received many feedbacks about improving the verification system.

My Role:

UX Researcher

Tools and Technique:

Mixed Methods

Timeline:

2022-2023 (Project on/off for half a year)

Ref: the 2-layer verification system

Problems

We've been getting regular feedback from the community that they didn't find the check system fair nor transparent. But the checks we had in place were affecting how users make purchases. Also, new users were having a hard time figuring out how the system works. Therefore, we needed to make changes to how the verification system works.
Our customer support team has already come up with two ideas to replace the current verification system: One to change the wording of the current system for a minimal change; the other to use a badge system.

Goal

“As a user, I want to do my own research (DYOR) on a collection with a transparent and fair check system.”

Process

#1 Discovery & Research — Focus Group & Survey

Tools: Google Forms, Discord, Miro

  1. As a short-term goal, the focus was on the first proposal — upgrade the wordings of the verification system to have a quick solution. We gathered nameings through a workshop to make it simpler to understand. A focus group study was conducted to evaluate the perception of the current verification system and the new proposal.
  2. Creators were gathered via a quick call in Discord. We started with simple scalable questions that they could answer quickly in the chat. Small discussions conducted afterwards to gather further feedback/improvement ideas.
  3. In the meanwhile, a survey linked inside of a blog article was released for regular users to evaluate.

#1 Synthesis & Definition

Tools: Miro

  1. Synthesis from the focus group and surveys indicated that the new wording can slightly improve the unfair perception for the average users. However, creators still sensed the lack of clarity in the upgrade process, and they found it unfair because the fundamental process hasn't changed.
  2. Therefore, no big win from the short-term fixes. In fact, they might even cause confusion when we eventually make bigger changes to the system. Product team has decided to skip the quick fix and directly proceed with the second proposal.

#2 Discovery & Research — Survey

Tools: Google Forms

  1. The customer support team worked on enhancing the second proposal, using insights gathered from the initial research phase.
  2. A survey has been designed to gather user feedback about the badge system regarding overall impression, easiness to understand, fairness, transparency, the specific details of each badge's meanings and rankings.
  3. In the meanwhile, a survey linked inside of a blog article was released for regular users to evaluate.

#2 Synthesis & Definition

Tools: Miro

  1. The proposal garnered overwhelmingly positive agreement from both creators and non-creators, especially in terms of transparency.
  2. The specific badges has generated different responses regarding their importance, criterium and proof process.
  3. The outcomes has been presented through a workshop to continue refining the proposal.

  3. In the meanwhile, a survey linked inside of a blog article was released for regular users to evaluate.

Research Impact

  • Overall it enhanced the sense of fairness and transparency in comparison to the previous system, particularly for creators.
  • Over the course of the project, community members have been actively engaged in enhancing the product to align with their needs, embodying the essence of “User-Centered Design”.
  • A fruitful collaboration with the customer support team led to the integration of our insights, fostering a deeper understanding of each other's roles and strengths.
  • It empowered the users to do their own research!

Retrospective

  • The initial proposals originated from the customer support team , as they were the primary point of contact for frustrated creators. The research team was initially working separately as silo but joined in quickly afterward.
  • Throughout the process, the timeline and budget expectations kept changing, which added extra pressure on the UX/UI team to quickly gather feedback and complete the design. Clearer and more timely communication regarding the project scope is anticipated.”
