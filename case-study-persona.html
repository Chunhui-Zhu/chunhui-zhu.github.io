Introduction of Data-Driven Persona

Persona - Data Analysis

Overview

AtomicHub stands as an NFT Marketplace with thousands of users on WAX, EOS and IMX blockchain. As the busiest NFT marketplace in the world, we try to provide users the best NFT trading experience. Therefore, gaining a deep comprehension of our user groups holds utmost significance at this point.

My Role:

UX Researcher

Tools and Technique:

Mixed Methods

Timeline:

2022 April - 2022 October

Problems

Considering the substantial number of daily transactions taking place on our platform, our understanding of our users was too insufficient. This limited insight was causing us to misinterpret user requirements and data analytics. To enhance user experience according to their needs and boost the conversion rate, it's urgent that we gain a deeper understanding of our user base.

Goal

Identify and personify our current users based on their behavior and key attributes from data-driven perspective as opposed to only basic demographics.

Process

Discovery & Research — User Interviews

Tools: Confluence, Excel

  1. Interview Guidelines was defined from Stakeholder workshop, which gathered the expectation from different teams — how personas could assist each team's work and what relevant information we need.
  2. Community experts guided us in selecting different user types: play-to-earn gamers (who trade gaming NFTs frequently); collectors (focus on completing collectibles); Investors (mainly looking for profits); creators (generating and selling NFTs using our platform).
  3. 13x User Interviews with questions about discovery of the platform, their knowledge of the platform/NFT, NFT interests, duration/frequency of usage, usage routine, usage experience, competitor engagement, device preferences etc.

Synthesis & Definition — Workshop

Tools: Miro

Proto Personas was served as a starting point to formulate hypotheses, research questions and workshop guidelines.

  1. Synthesis: We sorted out traits from the user interviews into four main categories: Creator, Gamer, Investment-Focused Collector, and Passion-Driven Collector.
  2. Proto Persona showcase and workshop: We presented Proto Personas in a workshop to stakeholders, who we teamed up with to add colorful details to the persona descriptions, making it easier for everyone to memorize the proto personas.”

Ideation & Testing — Survey

Tools: TypeForm. Excel

  1. A comprehensive survey was launched across the website, using Typeform, to gather robust quantitative data. This data covered demographics (age, location, marital status, hobbies, jobs, etc.), psychographics (values, desires, goals, interests, lifestyle choices), and behavioral traits (usage patterns, time spent, occasions). We employed a mix of nominal (categorical), ordinal (ordered-category), and open-ended questions to gather this information.
  2. Qualitative Proto-Personas and Workshop Results were as a reference point for us and laid the groundwork for creating guidelines for our survey.

Development & Delivery — Data Process

Tools: R, Excel, Miro

  1. Processing and Clustering Data: We received over 1600 responses. After cleaning up the data, we visualised the basic distribution using Excel and employed K-Means model in R-Studio to cluster the cluster the data.
  2. Identifying Segment Membership and Key Attributes: Key attributes were pinpointed by comparing the average of the questions to the average of the clusters. These attributes encompass factors like main reason to collect NFTs, whether goal of completing sets, ownership, whether collecting enjoyable, whether main reason for investing, whether new games important etc.
  3. Personification: Based on the collected data, we identified four clusters among non-creators. These clusters were transformed into relatable stories, resulting in a total of five personas.

Launch & Beyond — Presentation

Tools: Figma, Excel, Miro

Started with organising a quiz contest featuring a range of engaging questions among the theme "Are You Familiar with Our Users?"—this was a strategy to draw attention towards the quantified Personas. During this session, we also introduced the five primary types of Personas to the audience from the whole company. Interestingly, we alsp discovered certain shared characteristics among the majority of users shown on the left side.

Outcomes

Impact & Retrospective

What has been achieved:

  1. Evoked curiosity regarding research methodologies and ontribute to expressing user perspectives within the product team.
  2. Facilitated the adoption of a more user-centered strategy, idea generation, planning, and collaboration by offering a clearer understanding of user profiles – for instance, our new feature Shopping Cart for Gamified Investors.

What can be done better:

  1. Incorporating surveys directly within the interface would be more effective than relying on external links, as it captured insights only from users who are willing to share their views.
  2. We should involve additional stakeholders throughout the process, rather than solely during updates.

Future work:

  1. User Persona is a living document that needs constant updates and optimisations.
  2. If possible, integrating survey data with behavioural insights from the data team can enhance user models, though this might require a larger budget and needs to address user sensitivities regarding wallet information.
  3. Many valuable product feedbacks were collected through the process, that should be integrated into an ongoing user feedback collection loop, enabling us to continually monitor user opinions.
